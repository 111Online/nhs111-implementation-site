111 online implementation documents

NHS 111 online is a new digital service, currently in public beta. Provided by NHS Digital it complements the national 111 telephone service. Users are triaged online using NHS Pathways clinical algorithms and referred to the most appropriate NHS service or given advice on self-care.

This page is where you'll find documents and tools to allow implementation of the 111 online product in your area.

Click on the documents listed below, to view or download.

Requirements Schedule v1.6

The Requirements Schedule is part of, and should be read with the End User Agreement.

The purpose of this document is to detail:

  • how to implement the 111 online service into a 111 provider or integrated urgent care service; and
  • various other operational requirements applicable to End User’s and their associated service providers, being GP practices, OOH GPs, 111 telephone service providers, walk-in-centres, A&Es, dental practices and other health care providers.

DoS & ITK Documents & Tools

ITK Testing Plan

As part of a succesful implementation, every service that receives cases from NHS 111 online via ITK messages needs to be tested with each DX code they are profiled for on DoS.

Testing must also be done when service providers change or when changing service configurations, such as implementing a Clinical Assessment Service (CAS).
